Oct 31, 1991 · The Paramount Theatre · Seattle, WA
Nirvana · The Paramount Theatre · Seattle

Halloween in the Palace of Splendor

Five weeks after Nevermind, the most important band in the world played their biggest show yet — for ten dollars a ticket, in their own city, in a room built for silent films.

The show wasn't even supposed to be here. It had been booked at the smaller Moore Theatre, but the unexpected commercial surge of Nevermind forced a last-minute move. Nirvana had outgrown a venue they hadn't even played yet.

Photographer Charles Peterson watched the label's film crew moving through the crowd and felt something shift. Six camera operators in black, he said, "reeked of money." Something was ending. But something extraordinary was being preserved.
